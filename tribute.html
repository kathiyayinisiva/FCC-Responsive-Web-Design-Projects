Dr. TSR Ramdhass

A loving grandfather & pioneer in bringing education to the masses in Palani, India

Dr. TSR Ramdhass or "Raju Thatha" as fondly called by his granddaughters, was a man who lived by example. Leaving behind a legacy that is unparallelled from his background, he continues to live on in every life he changed.

Dr. Ramdhass was born on October 26th, 1925 in a small town called Thevaram in South India. He grew up with a keen interests in education and developed a strong love for his native language, Tamil. He served in the Indian Navy and served during World War II immediately after finishing school. He was stationed in Mombasa and Rangoon during his service. After the war, he decided to pursue his education in liberal arts - he studied Philosophy in Pachaiyappa's College in Chennai. He further went on to finish his M.A. and also his Doctorate, from Australia.

Dr. Ramdhass joined Palaniandavar College of Arts and Culture as a lecturer and taught philosophy, Indian culture, history, English, Tamil and so many more subjects. He authored a book that is widely prescribed as a textbook today, titled 'History of Indian Culture'. During this period where he eventually was elected as the Principal and President of the college, he inspired and encouraged hundreds of students from his town to take up education, a time when farming was the way to go. He would go as far as provide free education, pay for college fees, provide students accommodation in his own house if student accommodation was not available.

He was always characterised as a man of his word, he was confident, honest and had integrity. He stuck to his principles and never harmed anyone even by his words. He was loving and outgoing and always ready to help. His generosity has changed lives and his legacy is a testimony to the same.

Post retirement, Dr. Ramdhass shifted to Salem, India where he taught spoken English. he also gave talks on All India Radio, and was frequently invited to give guest lectures and motivational talks in schools, colleges and educational events. In the last few years of his life, he lived with his family in Chennai and passed away on October 14th, 2008.

Even after 12 years of his passing and almost 100 years since he was born, Dr. TSR Ramdhass is loved, cherished, revered and remembered fondly by not just his family but also thousands of students whose lives he changed, the hundreds of colleagues who had the opportunity to work with him and the many more friends and family who were fortunate to have him in their lives.
